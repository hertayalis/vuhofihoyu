Aimee Carrero- Biography

Aimee Carrero is an American actress best known for voicing the main character Adora/Sge-Ra in the Netflix animated series She-Ra and the Princesses of Power. She also provided the voice of Princess Elena in the animated series “Elena of Avalor,” which aired on the Disney Channel. From 2014 to 2018, she starred as Sofia Rodriguez in the sitcom Young & Hungry and as Angie on the Cartoon Network’s live-action series “Level Up.”

Aimee Carrero- Professional Career

Carrero appeared in the 2009 film “Alvin and the Chipmunks: The Squeakquel.” She has also appeared on TV shows such as Greek, Baby Daddy, Lincoln Heights, The Mentalist, Men of a Certain Age, The Middle, and Zeke and Luther. Aimee has been cast as Angie in the Cartoon Network film “Level Up.” She reaffirmed herself in the television adaptation of the action film “Level Up,” which she ended up performing two seasons of in 2013.

Carrero appeared in the television film “Blue Lagoon: The Awakening” on the American television network Lifetime in 2012. That same year, she was cast in her first off-Broadway part, and in December 2012, she had the world premiere of “What Rhymes with America.” She was cast in the horror thriller “Devil’s Due” in 2014, and then had some recurring appearances in the second season of the FX series “The Americans,” where she played Lucia, a Sandinista independence fighter. She also appeared in the ABC family sitcom “Young & Hungry,” co-starring Emily Osment.

Carrero announced in January 2015 that she would be delivering her voice for Elena, Disney’s first Latin fairy tale princess for the series Elena of Avalor. The series later debuted on July 22, 2016. In 2018, she voiced the main character “Adora” in the Netflix animated series “She-Ra and the Princesses of Power.” She was cast in the part of Exandria Unlimited’s spinoff web series “Critical Role” in June 2021. She was also cast in the new part in Mark Mylod’s “The Menu.”
