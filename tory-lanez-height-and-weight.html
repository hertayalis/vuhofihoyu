Tory Lanez, whose real name is Daystar Shemuel Shua Peterson, is a Canadian rapper, singer, and songwriter born on July 27, 1992, in Brampton, Ontario, Canada.

He gained initial recognition with his mixtape “Conflicts of My Soul: The 416 Story,” released in August 2013. In 2015, he signed with Mad Love Records, a label founded by record producer Benny Blanco, through Interscope Records.

Tory Lanez’s debut studio album, “I Told You,” was released in 2016 and featured hit singles “Say It” and “Luv,” which reached numbers 23 and 19 on the Billboard Hot 100, respectively. He continued to make waves in the music industry, releasing his second and third studio albums, “Memories Don’t Die” and “Love Me Now?” in 2018.

In 2019, he released his fourth studio album, “Chixtape 5,” which achieved great success, peaking at number 2 on the US Billboard 200 chart. Throughout his career, he received recognition and honors, including a Grammy Award nomination for his song “Luv” in 2016 and four Juno Awards.

In 2020, he released his fifth studio album, “Daystar,” marking his first project after parting ways with Interscope Records. However, this album was accompanied by controversy as it addressed claims regarding his involvement in the shooting of rapper Megan Thee Stallion.

Tory Lanez has showcased his versatility as an artist, delving into various genres and collaborating with other musicians. He has remained active in the music industry, releasing singles and mixtapes through his own imprint, One Umbrella.

In addition to his music career, Tory Lanez gained popularity during the COVID-19 pandemic for hosting “Quarantine Radio” on his Instagram Live, engaging with fans and fellow artists during lockdown.

While Tory Lanez has faced legal issues and controversies throughout his career, his musical talents and ability to connect with audiences have solidified his position as a prominent figure in the rap and hip-hop scene.
