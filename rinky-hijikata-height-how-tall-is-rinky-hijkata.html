Rinky Hijikata height-Australian professional tennis player, Rinky Hijikata was born on February 23, 2001, in Sydney, Australia.

Hijikata was bor to Japanese immigrants, Junko Hijikata and Makota Hijikata. He started tennis when he was three or four years old. From 2013 until 2016, he went to The King’s School in Sydney.

His father instructs tennis. Growing up, Lleyton Hewitt and then Kei Nishikori were his two favorite athletes. From 2019 to 2021, Hijikata competed in collegiate tennis for the North Carolina Tar Heels men’s tennis team.

Rinky Hijikata career

Hijikata debuted in the ITF in March 2018 at the Australia F3 in Mornington, Australia. The next week in the Australia F4, he achieved his maiden victory.

Hijikata and Adrian Andreev of Bulgaria won silver in the boys’ doubles tennis event at the 2018 Summer Youth Olympics in October.

Hijikata received a wildcard in the men’s singles qualifying for the 2019 Australian Open in January 2019. He was defeated by Hiroki Moriya in the opening round.

Hijikata competed in the ITF tournaments in Mornington, Australia, in March, making it to the quarterfinals and semifinals. Hijikata earned his first professional singles championship at the M15 Fayetteville in Arkansas, United States, in September of the same year.

Hijikata received a wildcard in the men’s singles qualification for the 2020 Australian Open in January and advanced to the second round. He received a wildcard into the 2021 Australian Open – Men’s Singles qualifying in February 2021 as well, and he advanced to the second round.

In July 2021, Hijikata won his second and third ITF championships, giving him a total of four ITF World Tennis Tour singles victories for the year. As of November 22, 2021, his singles ranking was 369 going into the year 2022.

Hijikata qualified for the main draw of the 2022 Melbourne Summer Set 1 and made his ATP tour debut there in January 2022. In the final qualifying round, Hijikata defeated Henri Laaksonen, the world’s 98th-ranked player, to earn his maiden top-100 victory.

Hijikata entered the ATP Top 300 in April after winning back-to-back ITF competitions in California in March 2022.

After Rodrigo Pacheco Méndez, a Mexican wildcard rookie, retired in the round of 16, he earned a spot in the 2022 Los Cabos Open and advanced to the round of 16. This was his first ATP triumph. Daniil Medvedev, the world’s top seed and winner of his 250th match, defeated him.

On October 31, 2022, he claimed his first Challenger championship at Playford, Australia, rising 33 spots to No. 159 in the world. Since Alexei Popyrin, aged 19 years old, won the Challenger championship in Jinan, China, in 2018, he is the youngest Australian to have achieved this feat.

Hijikata received a wildcard to the Australian Open, where he defeated Yannick Hanfmann in a comeback triumph to win his maiden Grand Slam match. He was defeated by Stefanos Tsitsipas, the third seed, in the second round.

At the 2023 Delray Beach Open, he defeated the second-seeded team of Jamie Murray and Michael Venus to go to the doubles semifinals with American Reese Stalder.

He defeated Mexican competitors Hans Hach Verdugo and Miguel Angel Reyes-Varela to go to the final. He qualified for the Masters 1000 tournament at Indian Wells and beat Mikael Ymer in the opening round in straight sets. Second-round opponent Sebastian Baez, the 30th seed, defeated him.

He made his grass court debut in the Rosmalen Grass Court Championships and qualified for the main draw as a fortunate loser. He won his opening match against wildcard Gijs Brouwer.

He later overcame Marc-Andrea Hüsler after falling behind a set to win and go to his first ATP circuit quarterfinal. He defeated Mackenzie McDonald after falling behind a set again to get to his first ATP circuit quarterfinal.

He used a wildcard to go to the third round of the 2023 US Open (tennis), beating Marton Fucsovics and tennis player Pavel Kotov. He thus debuted in the rankings’ top 100.
